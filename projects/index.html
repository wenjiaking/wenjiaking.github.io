In utero intestinal metabolome

Symbiotic microbial colonization through the establishment of the intestinal microbiome is critical to many intestinal functions, including nutrient metabolism, and immune regulation. Whether a fetal intestinal microbiome exists is controversial, and whether microbially derived metabolites are present in utero is unknown. Two papers about whether human Placenta has microbiome have contradictory conclusions. In order to show whether in utero microbiome exists, one way is to determine whether bacterial DNA can be detected. However, due to some reasons, it is unlikely to amplify bacterial DNA from fetal intestines. An alternative way is showing the occurrence of microbiome byproducts to evident the existence of in utero microbiome. Some paper already shown that in utero human intestine harbors unique metabolome, including bacterial metabolites, which may inspire two hypothesis. One is the majority of microbial metabolites detected in the fetal cohort represent vertically transmitted metabolites produced by or in response to the maternal microbiota, while the other is some microbial metabolites could potentially be derived from or in response to microbes in the fetal intestine. In order to differentiate between these possibilities, new samples were collected from fetal intestine, meconium, villi and maternal decidua. In addition, 11 single cell data sets are gathered to facilitate deciding the source of bacterial metabolites by assessing the gene expression of their receptors and carrier proteins.

The first step is performing DE analysis for each pair of the four tissue groups to detect differentially expressed metabolites, followed by pathway enrichment analysis. Apply ANOVA for each bile acid and bacterial metabolites over the four tissue groups to infer their possible source. Finally, analyze the single cell data to quantify the expression level of receptors and carrier proteins for bile acids and bacterial metabolites in utero to justify their source. [Read More]

A detailed chart review of thousands of potential patients about their relevant treatment histories and present circumstances was collected pushed by this motivation. Constructing Predictive Model for Abalone Age

Abalone is a type of marine snail. Unfortunately, the important commercial and recreational value of abalones put their populations at risk. Overfishing and poaching have caused some species of abalone to be endangered. To rem- edy the situation, putting regulations on harvesting young individuals would be a suitable approach, and elucidating the ages of abalones is critical for this task. The traditional method, counting the number of rings through a microscope to determine the age, is tedious and time-consuming. Thus, a predictive model based on physical characteristics, which are easier to obtain, is desirable. Linear models are my main candidate models in this report. The pipeline of my model constructing is data exploration, preliminary analysis, model selection and conclusion. [Read More]

Analysis of House Rentals Posts from Craigslist Website

Craigslist is a website that allows people to post classified advertisements for free. These posts span a variety of subjects, including job postings, housing rentals, and item sales. Craigslist started in the San Francisco Bay Area and now serves 570 cities worldwide. Posts are grouped by city to make relevant ads easy to find.

This project analyzed Craigslist posts about house rentals in Los Angeles, Sacramento, San Diego and San Francisco Bay Area, and each post is a separate text file. The pipeline is (1) data preprocessing including data cleaning and feature extraction; (2) analyze pricing based on features; (3) further inference by considering geographical characteristics and demographic maps. Numerical Algorithms of Solving Linear Least Squares Problem

This project compares directly computing based algorithms and iterative algorithms of matrix computation in terms of accuracy, time and space complexity by both theoretical analysis and simulation experiments. It mainly contains three sections: (1) Research in algorithms of tensor decomposition, matrix factorization involving SVD and Cholesky decomposition, and theoretically analyze their convergence rate; (2) Implement iterative methods of solving normal equations, such as SOR, Krylov subspace methods and combine them with preconditioning methods when condition number is large; (3) Compare algorithms in terms of numerical stability and convergence by simulated matrices with varied condition numbers.
